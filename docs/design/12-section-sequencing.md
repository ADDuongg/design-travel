# Section sequencing

**Purpose:** Encode **order logic** for long-form experiences—why story comes before specs, where proof lands, when commercial closure happens.

**Audience:** AI agents assembling pages from sections; humans auditing narrative flow.

---

## Universal curve (editorial travel / hospitality)

For detail pages where trust and emotion matter:

1. **Immersion** — sensory hero + thesis + light metadata garnish.
2. **Orientation** — optional social/share + **structured fact summary** (meta strip conceptually).
3. **Editorial “why”** — multi-paragraph story before itinerary or inventory.
4. **Operational honesty** — inclusions/exclusions, policies, boundaries in **banded** or clearly separated blocks.
5. **Cadence / logistics flavor** — scheduling windows, rhythm copy—**human tempo**, not admin tables.
6. **Structured arc** — itinerary or step sequence using **timeline** cognition.
7. **Sensory proof** — gallery or imagery mosaic after the arc is explained.
8. **Qualitative social proof** — quotes / voices.
9. **Quantitative proof** — ratings and counts—often after voices when building empathy-first brands.
10. **Conditional enrichment** — optional blocks (e.g. food) **only if content exists**; navigation adapts.
11. **Context expansion** — culture/history as **cards**, not lecture.
12. **Planning / commercial adjacency** — how to book, inquire, or configure—after belief exists.
13. **Continuity** — related items + calm exit ramps (back to catalog, browse cities).

**Why this order:** Trust is emotional before it is numerical; operations before empathy feels cynical; planning before proof feels extractive.

---

## Adjustments by product type

| Product | Emphasis shift |
|---------|----------------|
| **Journey / tour** | Itinerary timeline central; food/context optional satellites. |
| **Hotel / room** | Story → **rooms/inventory** sooner than pure tours—sleep product needs earlier tangibility—but still after emotional lead. |
| **City guide** | Food → experiences → hidden layers; practical notes late or sidebar mentally. |

---

## Conditional sequencing rules

- **Omit sections** with no data—**and** remove them from any in-page nav.
- **Merge intent:** if two sections would both be thin, combine under one stronger heading rather than two hollow blocks.

**Why:** Sequence promises **meaning**; empty sections break the promise.

---

## List pages (discovery)

Preferred curve:

1. Hero framing **how curation works**.
2. **Mood collections** before filters—teaches taxonomy.
3. Browse controls + sort.
4. Grid—parity cards.

**Why:** Tools before taxonomy feel bureaucratic; taxonomy teaches language users need for filters.

---

## Home / magazine pages

Curve:

1. Immersion hero.
2. Small curated set (fast credibility).
3. **Thematic band** with elevated density.
4. Visual/trend sections for **variety of scanning**.
5. Spatial/tool layer once emotion is engaged.
6. Collections for **return intent**.
7. Closing practical or seasonal chapter—**utility as epilogue**, not opener.

---

## Anti-patterns

- **Reviews before any brand voice** on editorial destinations—unless audience is deal-only.
- **Pricing block before “why”** for premium positioning.
- **Duplicated intent** (two gallery-like sections back-to-back without distinct jobs).
