# Semantic grouping

**Purpose:** Define **which container shape matches which meaning**—so users recognize structure instantly and agents pick consistent patterns across projects.

**Audience:** Designers and AI agents; avoid naming specific UI kits—think **roles**.

---

## Grouping is a cognitive contract

Each grouping pattern implies:

- **How** users should read (linear scan, compare, or savor).
- **Whether** items are interchangeable or ordered.
- **How much** detail belongs inside one glance.

---

## Parallel equivalence → parity cards

**When:** Items are **alternatives at the same decision level** (three hotels, three dishes, three tours).

**Behavior:**

- Same card altitude, same interaction grammar.
- Image → title → supporting body → optional link.

**Why:** Parity communicates **choice**, not progression.

---

## Ordered progression → spine / timeline

**When:** Items have **sequence**, **duration**, or **dependency** (days, legs of trip, steps).

**Behavior:**

- Vertical spine with nodes or clear stepping cues.
- Each step: label (phase/time) → title → detail.

**Why:** Spine implies **causality and memory order**; grids imply **swap-ability**.

---

## Comparable attributes → label–value clusters

**When:** Users must **compare numbers and categories** quickly (region, nights, price band, rating).

**Behavior:**

- Quiet universal labels; louder values.
- Footnotes separated visually from headline values.

**Why:** This is **inventory cognition**—different from narrative paragraphs.

---

## Facets / tags → pills or chips

**When:** Many **lightweight binary or categorical** markers (amenities, vibes, dietary tags).

**Why:** Scannable breadth without implying order or mutual exclusivity unless styled as single-select.

---

## Bounded nuance → stacked bordered cards

**When:** Each idea needs a **title + short explanation** and belongs to a family (culture notes, policy bullets as separate thoughts).

**Why:** Keeps lecture-like content **chunked**—users can skip a card without losing thread.

---

## Mosaic emphasis → asymmetric media grid

**When:** Proof is **visual first**; order matters less than **impact hierarchy** (hero tile + supporting frames).

**Why:** Signals gallery / evidence—**not** a balanced marketplace grid.

---

## Single decisive island

**When:** One dense bundle must sit in an editorial flow—meta summary, booking summary, “how this works.”

**Why:** One island reads as **deliberate instrumentation**; many scattered facts read as **noise**.

---

## Anti-patterns

- **Timeline for unordered perks**—misleading metaphor.
- **Cards for single paragraphs** that could merge—fragmentation without payoff.
- **Pills for sentences**—density without structure.

---

## Transfer checklist

1. Are these items **swappable** (parity), **sequential** (spine), or **attributes** (label–value)?
2. Does the chosen pattern match how users will **decide**?
3. If users skip one chunk, does the next chunk still make sense?
