# Storytelling patterns

**Purpose:** Encode **narrative reasoning** for digital pages—how voice, sequence, and framing turn information into a journey.

**Audience:** Content strategists, designers, and AI agents generating copy + structure together.

---

## Editorial-first posture

**Principle:** The product sells **restraint and judgment**, not infinite choice.

Story patterns:

- **Magazine metaphor:** pages feel like **features** and **dispatches**, not databases with hero images.
- **Constraint as luxury:** explicitly name what is *not* included (noise, booking chrome, aggressive upsell) to sharpen identity—even if competitors hide the same facts.

**Why:** Travel decisions are emotional; “catalog honesty” without tone reads cold.

---

## Named arcs, not anonymous sections

Sections should be **angle-driven**:

- Bad: “Overview,” “Details,” “More information.”
- Better: “Why this route breathes,” “Essentials & honest boundaries,” “A measured arc, not a spreadsheet.”

**Why:** Titles teach **how to read** the block; generic labels waste the most valuable real estate on the page.

---

## Lead vs. body split

- **Lead (`storyLead` conceptually):** one breath—often sensory + outcome.
- **Body:** multi-paragraph expansion with **room to breathe** (short paragraphs, oral rhythm).

**Why:** Web users commit to the lead in seconds; the body rewards those who stay.

---

## Operational honesty as character

Policies, exclusions, and indicative pricing are **brand moments**:

- Frame as **honesty** and **human correspondence**, not legal defense.
- Pair every constraint with **why it exists** when space allows (weather, respect for residents, sustainability).

**Why:** Constraints delivered with empathy increase trust more than hiding them until checkout.

---

## Progressive disclosure by evidence

**Do not render empty prestige.** If a subsection has no content:

- Remove it from navigation and layout, **or**
- Replace with a **single honest card** explaining state and next best action.

**Why:** Templates that show hollow sections teach users that the brand is shallow.

---

## Continuity beats hard sell

Endings should offer **adjacent journeys** (“same posture”) and **return paths** to discovery—not only “Book now.”

**Why:** Editorial brands win **return visits**; hard closures feel extractive after long reads.

---

## Language textures (portable)

| Texture | Use when |
|---------|----------|
| **Sensory** | Hero, food, place |
| **Cadence / time** | Itinerary, scheduling windows |
| **Social proof as voices** | Quotes before stars—when empathy leads |
| **Inventory as correspondence** | Rooms, SKUs—human-sized descriptions |

---

## Anti-patterns

- **Encyclopedia voice** in culture blocks—_users skim_; cards and titles must carry meaning.
- **Irony without warmth**—can read as condescending in hospitality.
- **Uniform superlatives**—destroys credibility across all destinations.
