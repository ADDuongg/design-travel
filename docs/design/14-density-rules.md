# Density rules

**Purpose:** Balance **operational truth** (specs, policies, grids) with **visual breathing room** so pages feel premium, not administrative.

**Audience:** Designers and AI agents composing long-form and hybrid pages in any visual system.

---

## Density is information architecture, not pixel count

**High density** is acceptable when:

- Users **expect** scanning (amenities, filters, metadata).
- Content is **chunked** into scannable units.

**Low density** is required when:

- Users must **feel** or **believe** before they compare.

---

## The breath gap

After immersive heroes or emotional leads, insert a **breath gap** before heavy structures:

- Short orientation copy, **or**
- A single **meta summary panel**, **or**
- A visual break (spacing + section title) that explicitly pivots tone.

**Why:** Immediate dense tables after immersion trigger “advertisement + fine print” heuristics.

---

## Banded sections as density containers

Visually **band** (background shift + boundary) sections that are:

- Repetitive (lists, policies),
- Comparatively dry,
- Longer than ~2–3 screenfuls of prose equivalent.

**Why:** Bands signal **chapter change** and prepare users for a different reading strategy (scan vs. read).

---

## Islands for facts

Place machine-readable facts in **contained islands**:

- Rounded perimeter,
- Interior grid alignment,
- Quiet labels / louder values.

**Why:** Islands separate **inventory cognition** from **narrative cognition** without forcing two pages.

---

## Chunking strategies (prefer order)

1. **Pills / chips** — many lightweight labels (topics, amenities).
2. **Cards** — parallel items with equal importance (food spots, policies as separate thoughts).
3. **Timeline / spine** — ordered journey with milestones.
4. **DL / label-value grids** — comparable attributes (region, duration, price band).

**Why:** Raw paragraphs of specs are the highest-density, lowest-retention format.

---

## Rhythm inside dense bands

Even inside dense sections:

- Maintain **vertical rhythm** (consistent gaps between chunks).
- Use **micro-headings** or label styles so users can resume scanning after interruption.

**Why:** Dense + chaotic spacing feels careless; dense + rhythmic feels intentional.

---

## Imagery as palate cleanser

Place **full-width or large imagery** between stretches of operational content when the subject is sensory (place, food, rooms).

**Why:** Eyes reset; emotion returns before the next dense band.

---

## Anti-patterns

- **Wall of policies** in one paragraph—break into cards or lines with rhythm.
- **Tables for narrative content**—only use tabular layouts when comparison across rows is the job.
- **Equal visual weight for primary CTA and secondary facts**—users panic-click or flee.

---

## Transfer checklist

1. Can I scan this dense block in **under 30 seconds** and know what class of thing each line is?
2. Did I **earn** this density with prior context sections?
3. Is there a **lighter** section coming after to avoid fatigue?
