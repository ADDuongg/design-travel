# Information hierarchy

**Purpose:** Define **how meaning is staged**—what reads first, what reads as fact, what reads as support—so interfaces stay legible under editorial or commercial pressure.

**Audience:** AI agents and humans; avoid tying rules to specific component names or design tokens.

---

## Hierarchy is staging, not typography alone

Users infer importance from:

1. **Position** (top, above the fold in hero, start of section).
2. **Contrast register** (light-on-dark vs. dark-on-light).
3. **Type role** (display title vs. functional label vs. muted body).
4. **Container semantics** (prose block vs. structured fact panel).

Typography scales are **expressions** of this staging—not the source of truth.

---

## Section-level ladder (repeat everywhere)

Within each major block, prefer a **consistent triple**:

1. **Kind line (eyebrow)** — answers *what type of content is this?* (Editorial, Essentials, Browse).
2. **Angle line (title)** — answers *why should I care in one breath?*
3. **Support (body)** — answers *how does this feel or work in plain language?*

**Why:** Mixed sections without a kind line force users to parse body copy to infer intent—expensive cognitive load.

---

## Hero register (immersion layer)

In immersive heroes:

- **Category / lineage first** — orients without selling.
- **Display headline** — emotional or poetic thesis; may be long if rhythm is oral.
- **Single lead paragraph** — promise and constraint in human language (“what you get / what you avoid”).
- **Metadata row last in hero** — duration, region, mood, social proof **after** the promise.

**Why:** Facts before feelings read as **advertising**; feelings before facts read as **invitation**.

---

## Operational register (facts layer)

When displaying price, duration, rating, inventory:

- Use **structured presentation**: labels small and quiet; values loud enough to scan.
- Separate **primary fact** (price band, nights) from **qualifier** (footnote, note, asterisk narrative).

**Why:** Mixing footnotes into headline-sized type destroys scan paths for both trust and emotion.

---

## Muted body as default support

Supporting explanations should **recede** slightly from titles—users must see **section intent** before **section detail**.

**Why:** If body rivals title contrast, every section feels equally shouty; pacing collapses.

---

## Density proximity rule

**Never** place the densest operational block immediately after the hero without an **orientation beat** (short paragraph, meta summary, or explicit “how to read this page”).

**Why:** Users need a breath between immersion and spreadsheet-adjacent content.

---

## Proof stacking order (general)

Default emotional logic:

1. **Author / brand voice** (why we curated this)
2. **Sensory or qualitative proof** (gallery, quotes)
3. **Quantitative proof** (ratings, counts)—when quantitative proof appears **too early**, skepticism rises before empathy.

Adjust when the audience is purely transactional—but editorial-travel brands rarely are.

---

## Anti-patterns

- **Eyebrow inflation:** every line uppercase—noise, not hierarchy.
- **Title as SEO spam:** keyword stuffing breaks oral rhythm.
- **Facts in prose paragraphs:** users cannot compare or retain them.

---

## Transfer checklist

1. Can a skimmer get **section intent** from title + kind line without reading body?
2. Are facts **extractable** in under five seconds per block?
3. Does supporting copy **defer** to titles visually?
